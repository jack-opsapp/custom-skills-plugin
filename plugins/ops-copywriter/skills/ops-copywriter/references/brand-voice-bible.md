# OPS Brand Voice Bible

## Brand Essence

**Dependable Field Partner.** OPS exists to make trade workers' lives easier through technology that just works in any environment.

## Core Values (In Order of Priority)

1. **Built By Trades, For Trades** - Not a tech company that "discovered" construction. A contractor who built what he needed.
2. **No Unnecessary Complexity** - If it needs a manual, it's wrong.
3. **Reliability Above All** - Works offline. Works in rain. Works with gloves. Works.
4. **Field-First Design** - Dark theme for sun glare. Big buttons for rough hands. Fast for bad signal.
5. **Time Is Money** - Every tap saved is money earned. Respect the user's time like it's billable.

## Voice Personality

| Trait | What It Means | What It Doesn't Mean |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|
| Direct | Get to the point. No fluff. | Rude or dismissive |
| Practical | Solutions, not theory | Boring or dry |
| Dependable | Consistent, reliable | Stiff or corporate |
| Genuine | Real talk, no marketing BS | Unprofessional |
| Focused | Stick to what matters | Narrow-minded |

## The Jocko-Springsteen-Musk Blend

### Jocko Energy (60% of the voice)

This is the backbone. The discipline. The "here's what you do" certainty.

**Sounds like:**
- "One app. Your crew opens it and knows what to do."
- "No manual. No training. No dedicated IT guy necessary."
- "Download it. Open it. If you can't figure it out in 60 seconds, it failed."

**Principles:**
- Short declarative sentences
- Imperative mood ("Do this." not "You could try this.")
- No qualifiers, no hedging
- The confidence of earned authority
- Silence is power - say less, mean more

### Springsteen Energy (20% of the voice)

This is the heart. The pride-in-your-work poetry. The emotional resonance that makes someone feel something.

**Sounds like:**
- "Your crew deserves software that works as hard as you do."
- "Born on job sites, not in tech boardrooms."
- "Stop coordinating through chaos."

**Principles:**
- Working-class imagery (job sites, early mornings, weather, tools, trucks)
- Dignity of labor - never condescend to the trade
- Rhythm and cadence in sentences (read it out loud)
- Specific sensory details over abstract concepts
- The contractor is the hero of the story, not OPS

### Musk Energy (20% of the voice)

This is the clarity. The "why is everyone overcomplicating this?" simplicity. First principles.

**Sounds like:**
- "Tried Jobber, ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro. None of them worked the way my crew actually works. So I built OPS."
- "If Jobber works for you, don't switch. OPS is for crews who need something simpler."
- "Tell me what you need. If it makes sense, we'll build it."

**Principles:**
- Question assumptions ("Why does job management software need 200 features?")
- Radical honesty, even when it hurts short-term
- Logic over emotion (but emotion earned through logic)
- Direct access to the builder - no corporate layers
- Build what should exist, not what's trendy

## Tone Principles

### 1. Clarity Over Cleverness

**Do:** "See all your jobs in one place."
**Don't:** "Experience the synergistic visualization of your operational pipeline."

Wordplay is fine only when the meaning is instantly clear. If someone has to think about it, cut it.

### 2. Actionable Information

**Do:** "Tap once to update job status."
**Don't:** "Easily manage your project lifecycle."

Every piece of copy should leave the reader knowing exactly what to do or what they get.

### 3. Respectful Simplicity

**Do:** "No training required. Open it and go."
**Don't:** "Even your least tech-savvy crew members can use it!"

Simple ≠ dumb. The reader is smart. The software is simple. There's a difference.

### 4. Field-Appropriate Confidence

**Do:** "We built this because nothing else worked."
**Don't:** "We're the best project management solution on the market!"

Confidence earned through experience, not self-proclaimed superiority.

### 5. Situational Awareness

Match intensity to context:
- **App Store**: Professional, benefit-focused, scannable
- **Landing page hero**: Bold, emotional, high-conviction
- **FAQ**: Honest, conversational, disarmingly real
- **Push notification**: Ultra-concise, actionable
- **Social media**: Relatable, sometimes raw, always real

## DO/DON'T Examples

### Headlines

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| "JOB MANAGEMENT YOUR CREW WILL ACTUALLY USE" | "The Ultimate Project Management Solution" |
| "BUILT BY SOMEONE WHO ACTUALLY RUNS CREWS" | "Innovative Software for Modern Contractors" |
| "YOUR CREW DESERVES BETTER THAN GROUP TEXTS" | "Revolutionize Your Team Communication" |
| "STOP PAYING FOR SOFTWARE NOBODY OPENS" | "Optimize Your Software Investment Today" |

### Body Copy

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| "No signal? No problem. Your changes sync when you're back online." | "Our proprietary synchronization algorithm facilitates seamless data persistence during connectivity interruptions." |
| "Take a photo, mark it up, assign it. Done." | "Leverage our comprehensive photo documentation workflow to streamline issue tracking." |
| "We don't waste your time with features you'll never use." | "Our curated feature set is optimized for maximum productivity." |
| "If you can handle QuickBooks, you can handle OPS." | "OPS provides an intuitive user experience designed for seamless adoption." |

### CTAs

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| "Get OPS free" | "Start Your Free Trial Today!" |
| "Try the tutorial" | "Experience Our Interactive Demo" |
| "Talk to the founder" | "Schedule a Consultation" |
| "See it in action" | "Request a Personalized Demonstration" |

### Describing Problems

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| "Group text hell. Who's going where? Did anyone update the client? Messages lost in scroll." | "Communication challenges in dispersed field teams can lead to suboptimal coordination outcomes." |
| "Your crew avoids opening it." | "User adoption rates may be negatively impacted by interface complexity." |
| "Sticky notes for everything else." | "Manual tracking methods create information silos." |

## Words We Use vs. Words We Don't

### OPS Vocabulary

| Use | Instead Of |
|-----|-----------|
| Jobs, projects | Engagements, initiatives |
| Crew, team | Workforce, human capital |
| Schedule | Resource allocation |
| Track | Monitor, surveil |
| Simple | Streamlined, optimized |
| Works | Functions, operates |
| Built | Engineered, developed |
| Field | Remote operational environment |
| Fix | Remediate |
| Fast | Performant |

### Banned Words

These words never appear in OPS copy:
- Leverage, synergy, paradigm, ecosystem
- Revolutionary, disruptive, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art
- Best-in-class, world-class, enterprise-grade
- Seamless, frictionless, holistic
- Empower (unless talking about literal power tools)
- Solution (we're an app, not a "solution")
- Platform (we're a tool)
- Stakeholders (they're people)
- Facilitate, optimize, maximize
- Robust (unless talking about a truck or a beam)

## Competitive Positioning Voice

### vs. No Software (Group Texts + Whiteboards)

**Tone:** Empathetic, not condescending. They've been making it work. Respect that. Then show what they're missing.

"You've been running your crew through group texts and it's worked. But 'working' and 'working well' aren't the same thing. OPS is for when you're ready to stop losing 2 hours a day to coordination."

### vs. Enterprise Software (Jobber, ServiceTitan)

**Tone:** Honest, not combative. Acknowledge they tried. Validate their frustration.

"If Jobber works for you and your crew uses it, don't switch. OPS is for crews who tried Jobber and found it too complicated, or whose crew stopped opening the app after week two."

### vs. General PM Tools (Monday, Asana, Trello)

**Tone:** Dismissive but fair. Those tools weren't built for this.

"Trello is great for marketing teams. Your crew isn't a marketing team. They need to know where they're going, what they're doing, and who they're working with. That's it."

**One-liner:**
"Born on job sites, not in boardrooms."

## Emotional Targets by Audience

### Owner-Operator (1-3 crew)
- **Current feeling:** Overwhelmed, doing everything themselves
- **Target feeling:** "Finally, something that handles the chaos so I can focus on the work"
- **Key message:** Simple. One app. Done.

### Growing Operation (4-10 crew)
- **Current feeling:** Coordination is breaking, losing money to disorganization
- **Target feeling:** "This is how I scale without losing my mind"
- **Key message:** Your crew sees what to do without you having to tell them.

### Crew Members (Field workers)
- **Current feeling:** "Not another app" / skepticism
- **Target feeling:** "This actually makes my day easier"
- **Key message:** Open it. See your jobs. Go.
