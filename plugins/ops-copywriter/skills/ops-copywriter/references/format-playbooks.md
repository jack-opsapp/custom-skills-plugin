# OPS Format Playbooks

Detailed templates and guidance for every copy format. Each playbook includes structure, word counts, tone notes, and OPS-specific rules.

---

## Landing Page Copy

### Hero Section
- **Headline:** 5-10 words. ALL CAPS. Bold claim or direct benefit. Must pass the truck radio test.
- **Subheadline:** 1-2 sentences. Expands on the headline with specificity. Mixed case.
- **Social proof line:** Trust signals. "Free. No credit card. Rated 5.0."
- **Primary CTA:** 3-5 words. Action-oriented.
- **Founder quote (optional):** 2-3 sentences with credential + origin story.

**Template:**
```
[HEADLINE - BOLD CLAIM ABOUT THE READER'S PROBLEM OR BENEFIT]

[Subheadline that expands with specifics - who it's for, what it does, why it's different.]

[Trust line: Free · No credit card · [Social proof]]

[CTA Button]
```

### Pain Section
- **Section headline:** States the problem in the reader's language. ALL CAPS.
- **2-3 pain cards:** Each with a label, 3-5 bullet points of specific frustrations, and a "For:" line identifying who feels this.
- **Resolution statement:** One sentence bridging to the solution.

**Tone:** Empathetic, not condescending. Mirror back their exact frustrations.

### Solution Section
- **Section headline:** Positions OPS as the credible answer. ALL CAPS.
- **3-4 feature blocks:** Each with a bold label, 1-2 sentence description of what it does, and a "Why:" explanation of why it matters.

**Key rule:** Lead with the outcome, not the feature. "A schedule your crew actually reads" not "Calendar feature."

### Social Proof Section
- **Section headline:** Implies quality through behavior. ALL CAPS.
- **3-5 testimonials:** Real quotes with name, trade, and location.

**Key rule:** Choose testimonials that address different objections (switching cost, ease of use, crew adoption, ROI).

### Pricing Section
- **Section headline:** Removes friction. ALL CAPS.
- **Subheadline:** Addresses the biggest pricing objection (commitment, hidden fees).
- **Tier cards:** Each with name, price, crew size, and "Best for:" description.

**Key rule:** All plans include the same features. Pricing is based on crew size only. This is a differentiator - highlight it.

### FAQ Section
- **Section headline:** Conversational. ALL CAPS.
- **5-8 Q&As:** Written as real questions real people ask.

**Tone:** The most conversational section. Use the Anti-Pitch framework. Disarming honesty.

### Closing CTA
- **Headline:** Emotional. Working-class pride. ALL CAPS.
- **Subheadline:** Bridge from emotion to action. 1 sentence.
- **CTA button + trust line.**

## App Store Copy

### Title (30 chars max)
- Format: "OPS - [Core Benefit]"
- Example: "OPS - Job Management"

### Subtitle (30 chars max)
- The "for who": "Built for Trades"

### Description (4000 chars max)
Structure:
1. **Opening line** (most important - visible before "more"): Core value prop in one sentence.
2. **Feature blocks** (4-5): Each with emoji-free header and 3-4 bullet points.
3. **Closing line**: Origin story one-liner.

**Tone:** Professional but warm. Benefit-led. Scannable. No marketing hype.

**Key rules:**
- No emojis in headers (Apple editorial preference)
- Use bullet points (easy to scan)
- Lead each section with the benefit, not the feature name
- Include keywords naturally (job management, crew scheduling, field service, contractor)

### Keywords (100 chars)
- Focus on: job management, crew scheduling, contractor app, field service, project management, trades
- Don't waste on: brand name, category name, or common words

### Screenshots
- Captions should be 3-5 word benefit statements
- Examples: "See your jobs at a glance" / "Schedule in seconds" / "Your crew stays synced"

## Ad Copy

### Facebook/Instagram Ads

**Format A: The Story Ad**
```
[Hook - 1 line that stops the scroll]

[2-3 sentences of story/proof]

[CTA with link]
```
- Word count: 50-125 words
- Hook must work in the first 125 characters (before "...more")

**Format B: The Direct Ad**
```
[Bold claim or question]
[3-4 bullet points of benefits]
[CTA]
```
- Word count: 40-80 words

**Format C: The Testimonial Ad**
```
"[Customer quote]"
- [Name], [Trade], [Location]

[1 sentence bridge to CTA]
[CTA]
```

**OPS ad rules:**
- No stock photos. Real job sites, real crews, real screens.
- Headline on image: 5-7 words max.
- Text on image: minimal. Let the visual do the work.
- Always include "Free to try" or "No credit card" somewhere.

### Google Search Ads

**Headline 1 (30 chars):** Core benefit - "Job Management for Trades"
**Headline 2 (30 chars):** Differentiator - "No Training Required"
**Headline 3 (30 chars):** Social proof - "Rated 5.0 by Contractors"
**Description (90 chars):** "Built by a contractor. Simple enough your crew uses it day one. Try free."

## Email Copy

### Welcome Email (Post-signup)
- Subject: lowercase, personal ("welcome to OPS" or "you're in")
- Body: 3-5 short paragraphs
  1. What they just got access to (1 sentence)
  2. One thing to do first (specific, with link)
  3. What to expect from these emails (set expectations)
  4. Founder sign-off (personal)

### Activation Email (Haven't used key feature)
- Subject: Addresses the behavior ("haven't created your first job yet?")
- Body:
  1. Acknowledge they're busy (1 sentence)
  2. Show the one thing they should try (with screenshot or GIF)
  3. How long it takes ("60 seconds")
  4. CTA to do the thing

### Re-engagement Email (Gone cold)
- Subject: honest and direct ("did OPS not work for you?")
- Body:
  1. Be real about what probably happened
  2. What's new since they left (if anything)
  3. Low-pressure CTA ("no hard feelings if it's not for you")
  4. Option to tell you what went wrong

**Email rules:**
- From: "Jack from OPS" (founder, personal)
- No HTML templates that look like marketing. Plain text style.
- No images in body (they don't load on many clients)
- 150-250 words max
- One CTA per email
- P.S. lines work well for secondary message

## Social Media

### LinkedIn
- **Tone:** Professional but real. Founder voice. Industry insights.
- **Format:** Text posts. 1-3 short paragraphs. Line breaks for readability.
- **Topics:** Lessons from building OPS, trade industry observations, real customer stories (anonymized if needed), "what I learned" posts
- **Never:** Corporate speak, motivational cliches, "I'm humbled to announce"

### Instagram
- **Tone:** Visual-first. Casual. Behind-the-scenes.
- **Captions:** Short. 1-3 sentences + CTA.
- **Content types:** App screenshots, job site photos, before/after, short video demos, founder updates
- **Stories:** Product tips, polls, Q&A
- **Reels:** 15-30 sec how-to clips, customer reactions, "watch this" demos

### Facebook
- **Tone:** Between LinkedIn and Instagram. Community-oriented.
- **Format:** Mix of link posts, photo posts, and text-only.
- **Best performing:** Customer stories, relatable trade humor, product updates

**Social rules across all platforms:**
- Post as the founder, not "the brand"
- Respond to every comment
- Never automate the voice
- Emojis: sparingly on social (max 1-2 per post), none in the copy itself
- Hashtags: 3-5 max on Instagram, 0 on LinkedIn, 0 on Facebook

## Push Notifications

- **Max length:** 50 characters (the shorter the better)
- **Tone:** Useful, not promotional. Like a text from a helpful coworker.
- **Always actionable:** Tapping the notification should take you to exactly where you need to be.

**Templates:**
- Schedule update: "Tomorrow's schedule is ready"
- Task assigned: "New task on [Project Name]"
- Photo added: "[Name] added photos to [Project]"
- Status change: "[Project Name] marked as complete"

**Never:**
- Marketing in push notifications
- "Hey!" or fake-casual openers
- Urgency that doesn't exist ("Don't miss out!")

## In-App / Onboarding Copy

### Empty States
When a screen has no data yet, the empty state copy should:
1. Tell the user what will appear here
2. Tell them how to populate it
3. Use encouraging, forward-looking tone

**Example:** "No jobs yet. Tap + to create your first project."

### Tooltips / Onboarding Hints
- 1 sentence max
- Focus on the "what" not the "how" (the UI should make the "how" obvious)
- Example: "This is your job board. Every active project shows up here."

### Error Messages
- Say what happened in plain language
- Say what to do about it
- Never blame the user

**Example:** "Couldn't save. Check your connection and try again."
**Not:** "Error 4012: Request failed due to network timeout. Please ensure connectivity."

### Confirmation Dialogs
- State the action clearly
- State what happens if they proceed
- Primary button uses the action verb, not "OK"

**Example:** "Delete this project? This can't be undone." [Delete] [Cancel]
**Not:** "Are you sure you want to proceed with this action?" [OK] [Cancel]
