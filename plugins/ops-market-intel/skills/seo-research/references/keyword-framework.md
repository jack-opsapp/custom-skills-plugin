# Keyword Evaluation Framework

## Intent Classification

### Buyer Intent (Tier 1) — Highest Priority
Keywords indicating active evaluation or purchase decision.

**Patterns:**
- `[competitor] alternative` — Switching intent, already using competitor
- `[competitor] pricing` — Evaluating cost, comparison shopping
- `[competitor] vs [competitor]` — Actively comparing options
- `best [trade] [tool type] app` — Ready to choose
- `[trade] software reviews [year]` — Final research phase

**Content type:** Comparison pages, pricing pages, "switching from X" guides
**Conversion rate:** Highest (est. 15-36% for long-tail variants)

### Problem-Aware (Tier 2) — High Priority
Keywords indicating known pain but not yet solution-shopping.

**Patterns:**
- `contractor missed calls solution` — Urgent operational problem
- `crew scheduling app for [trade]` — Active tool search
- `replace spreadsheet contractor` — Ready to upgrade
- `field quoting app` / `on-site estimate tool` — Specific need
- `contractor dispatch software` — Function-specific search
- `how to manage [trade] crews` — Process pain

**Content type:** Problem-solution articles, feature explainers, case studies
**Conversion rate:** Medium-high (est. 8-20%)

### Vertical Long-Tail (Tier 3) — Growth Priority
Trade-specific queries with low competition and high specificity.

**Patterns:**
- `[niche trade] job management software` — Underserved vertical
- `[niche trade] scheduling app` — Vertical-specific need
- `best app for [niche trade] business` — Ready to buy, niche

**Content type:** Vertical-specific landing pages, industry guides
**Conversion rate:** Medium (est. 5-15%) but low acquisition cost

## Opportunity Scoring

Rate each keyword cluster on these dimensions (1-5):

| Dimension | Weight | What to Assess |
|---|---|---|
| Intent Strength | 5x | Transactional > navigational > informational |
| Competition Gap | 4x | Weak existing content = high opportunity |
| OPS Relevance | 3x | Can OPS credibly serve this need? |
| Search Volume Signal | 2x | Forum activity, Reddit threads, X engagement as proxy |
| Content Effort | 1x | How much work to create quality content? (inverse) |

**Score = (Intent × 5) + (Gap × 4) + (Relevance × 3) + (Volume × 2) + (Effort × 1)**

Prioritize keywords scoring 50+ for immediate content creation.

## Content-Type Mapping

| Keyword Pattern | Best Content Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `[X] alternative` | Comparison landing page | "OPS vs ServiceTitan" |
| `[X] pricing` | Transparent pricing page | OPS pricing with competitor comparison |
| `best [trade] app` | Listicle/roundup (own it) | "5 Best HVAC Scheduling Apps (2026)" |
| `[pain point] solution` | Problem-solution blog post | "How to Stop Missing 60% of Your Calls" |
| `[trade] software` | Vertical landing page | "OPS for Roofing Contractors" |
| `how to [trade task]` | Educational guide (top-funnel) | "How to Schedule a 10-Person Crew" |

## Trade Vertical Keywords

### Well-Served (high competition, attack via switching intent)
- HVAC: "HVAC scheduling software", "HVAC dispatch app", "HVAC business management"
- Plumbing: "plumber scheduling app", "plumbing business software", "plumber dispatch"
- Electrical: "electrician scheduling software", "electrical contractor management"

### Underserved (low competition, attack via vertical pages)
- Landscaping: "landscaping crew management", "lawn care scheduling app"
- Roofing: "roofing contractor software", "roofing crew scheduling"
- Pest Control: "pest control scheduling software", "pest control route planning"
- Cleaning: "cleaning business scheduling", "janitorial crew management"

### Blue Ocean (near-zero competition, first-mover advantage)
- Pool Service: "pool service route app", "pool maintenance scheduling"
- Fencing: "fencing contractor software", "fence crew scheduling"
- Concrete: "concrete contractor scheduling", "masonry crew management"
- Glass: "glass company scheduling software", "window installer dispatch"
- Painting: "painting contractor software", "paint crew scheduling app"
- Flooring: "flooring contractor management", "flooring crew dispatch"

## AI Overview Impact

As of 2026, AI Overviews appear on ~80% of informational queries. Strategy implications:

- **Avoid pure informational content** — AI Overviews steal clicks
- **Target transactional/comparison queries** — Less AI Overview coverage
- **Include structured data** — Pricing tables, comparison charts rank better
- **Own branded queries** — "[OPS] vs [competitor]" pages
- **Long-tail specificity** — More specific = less AI Overview interference
