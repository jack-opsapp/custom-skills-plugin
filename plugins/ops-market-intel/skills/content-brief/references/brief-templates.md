# Content Brief Templates

## Template 1: Comparison Page ("[X] vs [Y]" or "[X] Alternative")

```
# Content Brief: [OPS vs Competitor] OR [Competitor Alternative]

## Target Keywords
- Primary: "[competitor] alternative" OR "OPS vs [competitor]"
- Secondary: "[competitor] pricing", "[competitor] reviews", "best [trade] software"

## Meta
- Title: "OPS vs [Competitor]: Which [Trade] Software Is Right for Your Crew? ([Year])"
- Description: "Comparing OPS and [Competitor] for [trade] contractors. See pricing, features, and why crews prefer OPS over [Competitor]."
- URL slug: /compare/[competitor]-alternative OR /compare/ops-vs-[competitor]

## Structure

### H1: OPS vs [Competitor] — Honest Comparison for [Trade] Contractors

### Section 1: The Quick Version (above fold)
- 3-column comparison table: OPS | [Competitor] | Why It Matters
- Key differentiators only (price, deployment time, mobile, crew features)
- CTA: "Try OPS free — no demo, no sales call"

### Section 2: Who Is [Competitor] Built For?
- Acknowledge what competitor does well
- Identify their ideal customer (usually larger, enterprise, residential-only)
- Contrast with who OPS serves (crews of all sizes, multi-trade)

### Section 3: Where [Competitor] Falls Short
- Use SPECIFIC competitor weaknesses from intelligence:
  - [Weakness 1 with evidence/quote]
  - [Weakness 2]
  - [Weakness 3]
- Frame as "what [trade] workers told us" not attack copy

### Section 4: What OPS Does Differently
- Crew-first design (not admin dashboards)
- Transparent pricing (show actual numbers)
- Instant deployment (download today, use tomorrow)
- Glove-friendly mobile (56dp touch targets)
- [Trade-specific features]

### Section 5: Pricing Comparison
- Side-by-side pricing table with real numbers
- Include hidden costs (implementation, add-ons, per-user fees)
- OPS pricing clearly displayed
- CTA: "See full pricing"

### Section 6: Switching from [Competitor]
- How long does migration take?
- What data transfers?
- Support during transition
- CTA: "Start your switch — we'll help you migrate"

### Section 7: FAQ
- "Is OPS as feature-rich as [Competitor]?"
- "Can I import my data from [Competitor]?"
- "Is there a free trial?"
```

## Template 3: Problem-Solution Blog Post

```
# Content Brief: [Pain Point Article]

## Target Keywords
- Primary: "[pain point] for contractors" OR "how to [solve pain]"
- Secondary: Related long-tail queries from research

## Meta
- Title: "[Pain Point]? Here's What [Trade] Contractors Are Doing About It"
- Description: "[Stat about pain point]. See how [trade] contractors are solving [pain] without expensive enterprise software."
- URL slug: /blog/[pain-point-slug]

## Structure

### H1: [STAT OR HOOK ABOUT PAIN POINT]

### Intro (100 words max)
- State the problem with a number
- Acknowledge the reader's frustration
- Promise a practical solution

### Section 1: Why This Happens
- Root cause (spreadsheets, no system, wrong software)
- Real example from Reddit/forums
- Cost of inaction (lost revenue, wasted time)

### Section 2: What Most Contractors Try
- Common solutions and why they fail
- Competitor solutions and their limitations
- "We talked to contractors who tried [Competitor] and here's what they said"

### Section 3: A Better Approach
- Introduce OPS as the solution (naturally, not salesy)
- Specific feature that addresses this pain
- Real workflow: before vs after

### Section 4: Getting Started
- How to implement the solution today
- CTA: "Download OPS and try it on your next job"

### Sidebar/Callout
- Quick stat or pull quote
- Related content links
```

## Template 4: Pricing Transparency Page ("[Competitor] Pricing")

```
# Content Brief: [Competitor] Pricing Breakdown

## Target Keywords
- Primary: "[competitor] pricing" OR "[competitor] cost"
- Secondary: "[competitor] pricing per user", "is [competitor] worth it"

## Meta
- Title: "[Competitor] Pricing in [Year]: What It Really Costs (+ Alternatives)"
- Description: "[Competitor] doesn't publish pricing. Here's what it actually costs based on user reports, plus transparent alternatives."
- URL slug: /compare/[competitor]-pricing

## Structure

### H1: WHAT DOES [COMPETITOR] ACTUALLY COST?

### Section 1: The Real Numbers
- Published pricing (if any)
- User-reported costs (from reviews, Reddit, BBB complaints)
- Implementation fees
- Add-on costs
- Total first-year cost estimate

### Section 2: Hidden Costs
- Training/onboarding time
- Per-user fees that scale
- Features locked behind higher tiers
- Contract lock-in terms

### Section 3: Is It Worth It?
- For large teams (20+): maybe
- For small teams (2-10): probably not
- For solo operators: definitely not
- Honest assessment

### Section 4: Transparent Alternative
- OPS pricing (clear, published)
- What's included at each tier
- No hidden fees, no forced demo
- Side-by-side cost comparison table
- CTA: "See OPS pricing"
```
